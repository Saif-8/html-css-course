Main Content

This is the main content area. Notice that the content starts below the fixed header and to the right of the fixed sidebar. You can scroll this section, and the header and sidebar will remain fixed in place.

CSS positioning allows you to control the layout of elements in your webpage. The header uses position: fixed, so it remains at the top of the page even when you scroll. Similarly, the sidebar is also fixed, making it stay on the left side of the page as you scroll the content.

When using position: relative, elements are positioned relative to their normal flow in the document. This is useful when you need to adjust an element's position without removing it from the layout.

You can experiment with different positioning techniques, such as position: absolute to position elements relative to their nearest positioned ancestor, or position: fixed to make elements stay in place as you scroll.

Try resizing the browser window to see how the layout behaves. The header and sidebar remain fixed, while the content area adjusts to the available space.
